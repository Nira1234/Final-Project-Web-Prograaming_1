About Bangladesh

Bangladesh, officially the People's Republic of Bangladesh, is a country in South Asia. It is the eighth-most populous country in the world and twelfth-most densely populated with a population of 174,655,977[18] in an area of 148,460 square kilometres (57,320 sq mi). Bangladesh shares land borders with India to the north, west, and east, and Myanmar to the southeast. To the south, it has a coastline along the Bay of Bengal.

Sundarbans Mangrove Forest

The largest mangrove forest in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, home to the Bengal tiger.

Ahsan Manzil

Ahsan Manzil is known as the Pink Palace, this was the official residence of the Nawabs of Dhaka.

Cox's Bazar Beach

Cox's Bazar Beach (Chattogram) is the longest natural sea beach in the world, stretching over 120 km.

Ratargul Swamp Forest

Ratargul Swamp Forest (Sylhet)is the only freshwater swamp forest in Bangladesh, known for its unique biodiversity.

Ramna Park

Ramna Park is a significant urban green space renowned for its historical significance and recreational amenities

Sajek Valley

Sajek Valley (Rangamati) is a picturesque valley in the Chittagong Hill Tracts, famous for its clouds and tribal culture.

Mahasthangarh

Mahasthangarh (Bogura)is one of the oldest archaeological sites in Bangladesh. It is dating back to the Mauryan Empire.

Sonargaon

Sonargaon (Narayanganj) is the ancient capital of Bengal. There are showcasing the Panam City ruins and Folk Arts Museum.

Jaflong

Jaflong (Sylhet)is the famous for its stone collection, tea gardens, and the Dawki River near the Indian border.

Sports

Sports play a vital role in the cultural fabric of Bangladesh, with cricket being the most popular and widely celebrated game in the country. The Bangladesh National Cricket Team, also known as the Tigers, has gained international recognition, particularly after achieving Test status in 2000 and hosting prestigious tournaments like the ICC Cricket World Cup. Football, once the most beloved sport, also holds a significant place in the hearts of many, with local leagues and the passion surrounding matches creating vibrant scenes. Traditional sports like kabaddi, the national game of Bangladesh, continue to reflect the rural essence and heritage of the nation. Other sports, such as hockey, volleyball, and athletics, are steadily gaining prominence, with the youth showcasing their talents on various platforms. The country has also shown promise in sports like shooting and archery, earning medals in regional and international competitions.
